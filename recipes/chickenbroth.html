Chicken Broth

Simple Chicken Bone Broth

Behold! A simple recipe for Chicken Bone Broth that anyone can follow! This recipe produces a simple, yet highly nourishing, broth that you can add to any dish or enjoy on its own. For accessibility, this recipe uses a pre-roasted rotisserie chicken.

There's nothing quite like warm chicken soup when you're under the weather. Chicken broth is the base of many dishes across the globe. Learning to make this staple is a great foundation for great cooking.

Ingredients

  • A Whole Chicken
  • Water
  • 4 Tbsp Salt
  • 1 Tbsp Turmeric
  • 2 Tbsp Apple Cider Vinegar
  • 2 Whole Onions Chopped (4 Tbsp Powdered)
  • 3-5 Cloves of Garlic Chopped (3 Tbsp Powdered)
  • 3 Large Carrots with Skin
  • 3-4 Stalks of Celery
  • 1 Bunch of Cilantro Chopped

Prep

  1. Separate chicken into pieces, trim fat if desired
  2. Chop veggies into thick chuncks and sice carrots
  3. Place chicken and chopped veggies into a large pot, add water until water is an inch below rim
  4. Add Apple Cider Vinegar and powdered ingredients

Cooking Steps

  1. Place pot on stove over high heat, cover with lid, allowing space for steam to escape
  2. Bring to a boil then reduce to medium high heat
  3. Cover fully and cook on medium high heat for about 3 hours, stirring occasionally
  4. Remove chicken and separate meat from bones
  5. Place bones back in pot and add 1-2 cups of water
  6. Reduce heat to low, let simmer overnight or 24hrs
  7. When bones fall apart remove vegetables
  8. Line colander with a cheesecloth and strain broth into a clean bowl
  9. Pour finished broth into Mason Jars or airtight container leaving 1/2 inch of space between broth and lid
  10. Close lid while broth is warm
  11. Store in freezer or fridge after broth has cooled
